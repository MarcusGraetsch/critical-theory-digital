## Key Decisions Needed from Marcus

### 1. Scope of Butler
**Question:** How central should Butler's performativity be to the project?

**Options:**
- **Full integration:** Gender/identity performance as paradigmatic for all digital identity construction (3,000-word paper)
- **Selective use:** Reference Butler in passing when discussing identity curation, but don't give her a dedicated chapter
- **Exclude:** Focus on the Marxist-phenomenological strand (Hegelâ†’Marxâ†’LukÃ¡csâ†’Debordâ†’Baudrillard)

**Recommendation:** Full integration if the project aims for interdisciplinary reach; selective use if staying closer to political economy/critical theory tradition.

---

### 2. Empirical Grounding
**Question:** How much empirical research should ground the theoretical claims?

**Options:**
- **Pure theory:** Conceptual analysis only, illustrated with anecdotes
- **Light empirics:** Existing platform studies, journalism, reports cited as support
- **Medium empirics:** Rook conducts light research (subreddit ethnography, platform documentation, user testimonials)
- **Heavy empirics:** Marcus conducts or commissions interviews, surveys (probably out of scope)

**Recommendation:** Light to medium empiricsâ€”enough that claims don't feel armchair-philosophical, but not so much that the project becomes an empirical study.

---

### 3. Normative Stance
**Question:** How critical vs. descriptive should the final synthesis be?

**Options:**
- **Descriptive:** "This is how digital existence is structured" (neutral)
- **Immanent critique:** "Digital platforms promise X but structurally undermine it" (shows internal contradictions)
- **External critique:** "Digital existence is alienated/authentic, bad/good" (judges against external norm)

**Recommendation:** Immanent critiqueâ€”shows how the digital promises connection/freedom/authenticity but structurally prevents them. More rigorous than external moralizing.

---

### 4. Final Output Format
**Question:** What is the intended audience and format?

**Options:**
- **Academic article:** 8,000-10,000 words, proper citations, submit to journal (New Media & Society? Theory, Culture & Society?)
- **Long-form essay:** 15,000-20,000 words, magazine-style (Logic Magazine? Real Life?)
- **Book manuscript:** 60,000+ words, full book proposal
- **Series of blog posts:** Modular, easier to publish, harder to synthesize
- **Something else:** Podcast scripts? Video essays?

**Recommendation:** Start with long-form essay (publishable as standalone), then assess whether there's enough for a book. Academic article too narrow; blog series loses the synthetic argument.

---

### 5. The AI Question
**Question:** Does the recent AI turn (LLMs, 2022-present) constitute a new break, or an intensification of the participative turn?

**Options:**
- **New break:** AI represents a qualitative shift (synthetic relationships, not just mediated ones)
- **Intensification:** AI extends existing logics (platform capture, data extraction, participative performance)
- **Undecided:** Let the case study determine this

**Implication:** If new break, need dedicated theoretical treatment beyond the case study. If intensification, fold into existing framework.
