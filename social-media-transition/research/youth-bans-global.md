# Youth Social Media Bans — Global Overview

**Status:** Research Phase  
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-14

---

## Australia 🇦🇺

### Legislation
- **Status:** Passed November 2024
- **Age Limit:** Under 16
- **Implementation:** Trial phase ongoing
- **Platforms Covered:** TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, X, Facebook
- **Enforcement:** Age verification required, parental consent insufficient

### Key Provisions
- Platforms face fines up to AUD $49.5M for non-compliance
- Government to test age verification technologies
- Full implementation expected late 2025

## United States 🇺🇸

### Federal Level

#### KOSA — Kids Online Safety Act
- **Status:** Senate passed July 2024 (91-3)
- **House Status:** Stalled, reintroduced 119th Congress (May 2025)
- **Key Provisions:**
  - "Duty of care" for platforms
  - Disable "addictive" design features for minors
  - Parental control tools required
  - Opt-out from algorithmic recommendations
  - Highest privacy settings by default

**Controversy:**
- Criticized by EFF, ACLU for censorship risks
- Heritage Foundation wants to use it against LGBTQ content
- Civil liberties groups oppose ("Don't Delete Art" coalition)
- LGBTQ groups initially opposed, some dropped after 2024 revisions

#### COPPA 2.0
- Updates to 1998 Children's Online Privacy Protection Act
- Raises age of protection from 13 to 16
- Bans targeted advertising to minors

### State Level

| State | Legislation | Status | Key Points |
|-------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Utah** | SB 152 (2023) | Enacted | Parental consent for minors' accounts |
| **Arkansas** | SB 396 | Enacted | Age verification required |
| **Louisiana** | Act 442 | Enacted | ID verification for adult content |
| **Texas** | HB 18 | Enacted | Parental consent, algorithm transparency |
| **California** | AB 2273 | Enacted | Age-appropriate design code |
| **Ohio** | Pending | Proposed | Social media ban under 16 |
| **Florida** | HB 1 | Passed | Ban under 16 (challenged in court) |

**Court Challenges:**
- Multiple state laws struck down as unconstitutional
- NetChoice v. Moody precedent affects enforcement
- First Amendment concerns central to challenges

## United Kingdom 🇬🇧

### Online Safety Act (2023)
- **Regulator:** Ofcom
- **Age Assurance:** Required by platforms
- **Protection:** Under-18s from harmful content
- **Enforcement:** Fines up to 10% global turnover

### Implementation Timeline
- 2024: Codes of practice published
- 2025: Full compliance expected
- Focus: Age verification, parental controls

---

## European Union 🇪🇺

### Digital Services Act (DSA)
- **Age Requirements:** Platforms must protect minors
- **Algorithmic Transparency:** Required disclosures
- **Targeted Ads:** Restricted for minors
- **Enforcement:** National regulators, EU Commission

### Member State Developments
- **France:** Age verification pilots
- **Germany:** JMStV (youth media protection) under revision
- **Netherlands:** Kijkwijzer system for social media
- **Ireland:** DSA enforcement hub (Meta, Google, TikTok based there)

---

## Germany 🇩🇪

### Current Framework
- **JMStV:** Jugendmedienschutz-Staatsvertrag
- **KJM:** Kommission für Jugendmedienschutz
- **Age Ratings:** USK extending to online content

### Proposed Changes
- Stricter age verification for social platforms
- Extension of youth protection to algorithmic feeds
- Parental control mandates

### di.day Initiative
- **Website:** https://www.di-day.de/
- **Focus:** Digital exchange day in schools
- **Goal:** Alternative to algorithm-driven consumption
- **Target:** Digital literacy, mindful tech use

## Asia-Pacific

### China 🇨🇳
- **TikTok (Douyin):** Youth mode limits usage to 40 min/day for under-14s
- **Gaming:** Strict playtime limits for minors
- **Regulator:** National Press and Publication Administration

### South Korea 🇰🇷
- Shutdown law (Cinderella Law) largely repealed
- Shift to parental choice model
- Still strict compared to Western standards

## Impact Assessment

### Platform Revenue Risk
- **High:** TikTok, Instagram (youth demographics)
- **Medium:** Snapchat, YouTube
- **Lower:** LinkedIn, Facebook (older demographics)

### Age Verification Technologies
- **Document Upload:** Privacy concerns
- **AI Face Analysis:** Accuracy issues, bias
- **Parental Verification:** Circumvention easy
- **Third-Party Services:** Cost, data sharing

### Unintended Consequences
- Youth moving to unregulated platforms
- VPN usage increase
- Privacy erosion from age verification
- Exclusion of unverified users
