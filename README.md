# In Search of Meaning in Cyberspace

*Digital Escapism, Alienation, and the Spectacle of the 21st Century*

## Core Hypothesis

As material conditions under contemporary capitalism make traditional forms of freedom (homeownership, mobility, family formation) increasingly unaffordable, humans retreat into digital spaces—social media bubbles, AI companionships, virtual worlds—as substitutes for fulfillment. These digital engagements function as:

- **Escapism** – fleeing an economically foreclosed material reality
- **Sublimation** – redirecting blocked desires into digital proxies
- **Coping mechanisms** – filling the void left by atomization and precarity
- **False consciousness** – mistaking the simulacrum for the real

## Theoretical Framework

| Thinker | Concept | Application |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Hegel** | *Verkehrte Welt* (inverted world), *Schein* (appearance) | The digital as reality turned inside-out—where the representation precedes and replaces the thing itself |
| **Marx** | *Entfremdung* (alienation) | Labor alienation transposed onto digital self-commodification; we produce our own alienation through content creation |
| **Lukács** | *Verdinglichung* (reification) | Social relations becoming thing-like in algorithmic feeds—humans as data points engaging with other data points |
| **Debord** | *Spectacle* | Not just images we watch, but environments we inhabit; total colonization of life by representation |
| **Baudrillard** | *Simulacrum* | Digital spaces where the map precedes the territory—hyperreality as the only reality we can afford |
| **Butler** | *Performativity* | Identity construction in digital spaces; the curated self as ongoing performance for algorithmic audiences |

## Research Questions

1. How do digital spaces function as *compensatory* mechanisms for material deprivation?
2. In what ways has the "digital" become not just escapism but the *primary* site of meaning-making?
3. Can we distinguish between "authentic" digital engagement and alienated digital labor?
4. What forms of resistance or escape are possible *within* the digital, vs. requiring exit from it?
