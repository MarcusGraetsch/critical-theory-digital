# Additional Empirical Sources: Creator Economy and Platform Studies

*Related studies and ongoing research to be integrated*

## Core References

### Creator Economy / Influencer Labor

**Duffy, Brooke Erin. (2017).** *(Not) Getting Paid to Do What You Love: Gender, Social Media, and Aspirational Work*. New Haven: Yale University Press.
- Focus on gendered dimensions of influencer labor
- "Aspirational work" concept: unpaid labor performed for future opportunity
- Platform dependence and precarity

**Cunningham, Stuart, & Craig, David. (2019).** *Social Media Entertainment: The New Intersection of Hollywood and Silicon Valley*. New York: NYU Press.
- "SME" as distinct industry
- Platform governance and creator relations
- Professionalization of creator labor

**Bishop, Sophie. (2018).** "Anxiety, panic and self-optimization: Inequalities and the YouTube algorithm." *Convergence*, 24(6), 589-605.
- Algorithm as source of creator anxiety
- Self-optimization as response to opacity
- Gendered and racialized inequalities in visibility

**Duffy, B. E., & Hund, E. (2019).** "'Having it All' on Social Media: Entrepreneurial Femininity and Self-Branding Among Fashion Bloggers." *Social Media + Society*, 5(1).
- Self-branding as gendered labor
- Authenticity performance
- Entrepreneurial subjectivity

---

## Platform Studies

**Gillespie, Tarleton. (2018).** *Custodians of the Internet: Platforms, Content Moderation, and the Hidden Decisions That Shape Social Media*. New Haven: Yale University Press.
- Content moderation as governance
- Platform accountability
- Transparency and opacity

**Zuboff, Shoshana. (2019).** *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*. New York: PublicAffairs.
- Data extraction as economic model
- Behavioral modification
- "Surveillance capitalism" as distinct from industrial capitalism

**Pasquale, Frank. (2015).** *The Black Box Society: The Secret Algorithms That Control Money and Information*. Cambridge: Harvard University Press.
- Opacity of algorithmic systems
- Accountability gaps
- "Black box" as power mechanism

**Srnicek, Nick. (2017).** *Platform Capitalism*. Cambridge: Polity Press.
- Platform as business model
- Different platform types (advertising, cloud, industrial, etc.)
- Monopoly tendencies

---

## Digital Labor / Gig Economy

**Woodcock, Jamie, & Graham, Mark. (2020).** *The Gig Economy: A Critical Introduction*. Cambridge: Polity Press.
- Platform labor beyond content creation
- Comparative analysis across gig sectors
- Resistance and organization

**van Doorn, Niels. (2017).** "Platform labor: on the gendered and racialized exploitation of low-income service work in the 'on-demand' economy." *Information, Communication & Society*, 20(6), 898-914.
- Intersectional analysis of platform labor
- Gendered and racialized dimensions
- Service work vs. creative work

**Rosenblat, Alex. (2018).** *Uberland: How Algorithms Are Rewriting the Rules of Work*. Berkeley: University of California Press.
- Algorithmic management
- Driver experiences of opacity
- Gamification and control

---

## Psychological / Phenomenological Studies

**Twenge, Jean M. (2017).** *iGen: Why Today's Super-Connected Kids Are Growing Up Less Rebellious, More Tolerant, Less Happy*. New York: Atria Books.
- Mental health correlations with smartphone adoption
- Loneliness despite connectivity
- Generational shifts

**Andreassen, C. S., Torsheim, T., & Pallesen, S. (2014).** "Predictors of use of social network sites at work." *Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication*, 19(4), 906-921.
- Social media addiction scales
- Workplace distraction
- Compulsive use patterns

**Frost, R. L., & Rickwood, D. J. (2017).** "A systematic review of the mental health outcomes associated with Facebook use." *Computers in Human Behavior*, 76, 576-600.
- Meta-analysis of Facebook and mental health
- Social comparison mechanisms
- Anxiety and depression correlations

---

## Algorithm Studies

**Bucher, Taina. (2018).** *If...Then: Algorithmic Power and Politics*. Oxford: Oxford University Press.
- Algorithm as social power
- Facebook's News Feed specifically
- User experiences of algorithms

**Eslami, Motahhare, et al. (2016).** "'I always assumed that I wasn't really that close to [her]': Reasoning about invisible algorithms in news feeds." *Proceedings of CHI 2016*.
- User awareness (or lack thereof) of algorithms
- Social implications of algorithmic curation
- Folk theories in practice

**Gorwa, Robert. (2019).** "The platform governance triangle: conceptualising the informal regulation of online content." *Internet Policy Review*, 8(2).
- Governance beyond formal rules
- Multi-stakeholder approaches
- Platform power

---

## Critical Theory / Media Theory

**Dean, Jodi. (2009).** *Democracy and Other Neoliberal Fantasies: Communicative Capitalism and Left Politics*. Durham: Duke University Press.
- "Communicative capitalism"
- Participation as capture
- Fantasy of digital democracy

**Berardi, Franco "Bifo". (2009).** *The Soul at Work: From Alienation to Autonomy*. Los Angeles: Semiotext(e).
- Italian autonomist perspective on cognitive capitalism
- "Soul" as exploited resource
- Precarity and mental health

**Stiegler, Bernard. (2010).** *Taking Care of Youth and the Generations*. Stanford: Stanford University Press.
- "Disorientation" of digital life
- Attention destruction
- Pharmacology of technology
